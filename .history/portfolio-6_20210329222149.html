Enigma Emulation

Progressive substition code cypher based on WWII era Enigma code machines.

RollieBot was created for a design class at Berkeley, and was designed to possess exaggerated wide proportions. The vehicle went through several varying fidelity prototypes before arriving at its final iteration, made of lasercut plywood. The microcontroller inside RollieBot is bluetooth enabled, and thus can be controlled by smartphone. RollieBot's chassis was designed to be accessible as possible, making replacing or tweaking parts an ease.
